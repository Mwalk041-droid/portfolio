Personal Narrative

The Leap of Faith That
Changed Everything

A look at how I got here and why cybersecurity was the right direction from the start.

I came to Old Dominion University and chose cybersecurity after realizing it aligned with how I think about technology. I was drawn to understanding how systems work and, more importantly, how they fail. Cybersecurity sits at a unique intersection of technical knowledge, risk assessment, and organizational responsibility, and that combination kept me engaged in a way that other fields did not.

ODU's program aligned with how I learn best. The curriculum emphasizes hands-on engagement with tools, simulations, and lab environments rather than relying solely on readings and papers. Running vulnerability scans, identifying misconfigurations, and seeing how small errors could be exploited made the field come alive. It is constantly evolving, which keeps the work challenging and relevant.

Building Skills Through Experience

As I progressed through coursework, I recognized a gap in purely technical thinking. Many cybersecurity decisions are not about what is technically possible but about what is acceptable given cost, probability, and organizational impact. This led me to add a minor in Risk Management and Insurance, which introduced concepts like risk appetite, loss exposure, and mitigation strategies. I started approaching security problems less in terms of perfect protection and more in terms of managed risk and informed trade-offs.

I also built skills through hands-on projects including penetration testing labs using Kali Linux and Metasploitable2. Working with tools like Nmap and Metasploit helped me understand attacker methodologies and their defensive implications. Core concepts like least privilege, defense in depth, and attack surfaces became practical considerations rather than abstract definitions.

Internship and Real-World Work

My internship at the Earth Viability Center was a significant professional experience. Working as a cybersecurity analyst intern, I gained exposure to real-world security considerations, documentation practices, and collaborative work environments that classroom instruction could not fully replicate. I frequently translated security risks into practical recommendations for non-technical stakeholders, which strengthened my ability to bridge technical findings and business decisions.

My role as an Esports Arena Technician at ODU also contributed to my professional development. Working with live systems, managing user access, maintaining hardware, and troubleshooting under time pressure reinforced that availability and usability are just as important as security.

Certifications and Looking Forward

Earning the CompTIA Security+ validated my foundational knowledge across networking, security principles, risk management, and incident response. My AWS Cloud Practitioner certification expanded my understanding of cloud environments, particularly the shared responsibility model and the risks associated with cloud misconfigurations. I am currently preparing for the CompTIA CySA+, which focuses on the analyst mindset including threat intelligence, vulnerability management, and security operations.

My career goal is to work as a cybersecurity analyst in the defense contracting sector, in environments where security, compliance, and risk management support critical missions. As I prepare to graduate in May 2026, my education at ODU has been about developing a professional identity grounded in technical competence, risk awareness, and the ability to communicate across disciplines.
